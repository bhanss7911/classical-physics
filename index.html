Newton's Laws - Example Problems

Newton's First Law

Problem 1: A book rests on a table. A person applies a force of 10 N to push the book horizontally, but it doesn't move because of friction. What is the magnitude of the frictional force acting on the book?

Solution: Since the book doesn't move, the frictional force must equal the applied force:

\[ F_{\text{friction}} = 10 \, \text{N} \]

Problem 2: A car is moving at a constant velocity of 20 m/s. If no additional forces act on it, what will its velocity be after 10 seconds?

Solution: According to Newton's First Law, if no net force acts on the car, its velocity remains constant:

\[ v = 20 \, \text{m/s} \]

Newton's Second Law

Problem 3: A 5 kg object experiences a net force of 15 N. What is the acceleration of the object?

Solution: Using Newton's Second Law, \( F = ma \):

\[ a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{15}{5} = 3 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

Problem 4: A truck of mass 2000 kg is accelerating at \( 2 \, \text{m/s}^2 \). What is the net force acting on the truck?

Solution: Using \( F = ma \):

\[ F = 2000 \times 2 = 4000 \, \text{N} \]

Problem 5: A force of 50 N is applied to a 10 kg box, but friction exerts a force of 20 N in the opposite direction. What is the box's acceleration?

Solution: The net force is \( F_{\text{net}} = F_{\text{applied}} - F_{\text{friction}} \):

\[ F_{\text{net}} = 50 - 20 = 30 \, \text{N} \]

Using \( F = ma \):

\[ a = \frac{F_{\text{net}}}{m} = \frac{30}{10} = 3 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

Problem 6: A 2 kg ball is dropped from rest. Assuming no air resistance, what is the net force acting on the ball?

Solution: The force acting on the ball is due to gravity:

\[ F = mg \]

\[ F = 2 \times 9.8 = 19.6 \, \text{N} \]
